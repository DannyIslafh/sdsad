Introduction

This dashboard presents an analysis of welding process data including current, voltage, power, heat input, resistance, and voltage drop. The purpose is to understand electrical behavior in welding operations and evaluate performance trends.

Objective

  • To analyze welding electrical parameters
  • To study relationship between current and power
  • To evaluate heat input variation
  • To visualize data for better decision making

Industrial Diagnostic Equipment & Sensors

01 / Clamp Meter

Measures welding current (A) and arc voltage (V) non-intrusively during active arc state for power computations.

02 / Thermal Camera

Captures infrared radiation to map surface temperatures of the weld pool and Heat Affected Zone (HAZ) at ε=0.95.

03 / Arc Welding Analyzer

Detects dynamic physical reaction loads (N) resulting from expanding base metal under intense arc constraints.

04 / LVDT Sensor

Tracks sub-micron linear displacement (mm) to verify final structural geometric shrinkage distortions after cooling.

Key Findings

  • Higher current increases power output significantly
  • Voltage remains relatively stable across samples
  • Heat input increases proportionally with power
  • Resistance values slightly decrease at higher current

Conclusion

The welding dataset shows a clear relationship between electrical parameters. Increasing current leads to higher power and heat input, which directly affects welding performance. This analysis helps optimize welding conditions for better efficiency and stability.
